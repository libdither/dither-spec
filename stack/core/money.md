# 6 · Non-Concentrating Money

> 🚧 **Draft (core-idea sketch) — new design.** Supersedes the earlier "demurrage on a single fixed-supply token" sketch (and the deleted `applications/dither-currency.md`). *Part A · Core Ideas.*

**The problem.** Both the resource market and the truth machine degrade as wealth concentrates — "dispersed capital" (A2) is a *load-bearing assumption* of the truth machine, and nothing yet enforces it. Money left alone concentrates. We need a medium of exchange that (a) structurally resists concentration, (b) is denominated in something *real* and *local*, and (c) needs no global ledger and no fixed supply.

**The core idea.** There is no global "value" — only **value to a constituent**, exactly as there is no global truth, only per-resolver verdicts. So money is not a fixed-supply token. It is a **portfolio of fractional shares (equity) in local resource pools.**

- A **community / zone** `z` is a region of the [latency-trust coordinate space](../living-system.md). Its **pool** is its real productive capacity — storage, bandwidth, compute — valued in the [resource basket](resource-market.md): `V_z`.
- You hold a **share** `θ_{i,z}` of the pools you participate in, with `Σ_i θ_{i,z} = 1` (*fragments of a whole*). Your real wealth is the portfolio `w_i = Σ_z θ_{i,z}·V_z`. **Supply is never minted — it *is* the real value of the pools**, and grows exactly when capacity comes online. This dissolves the "how much money to print" question.
- Value is **local and must physically propagate.** The exchange rate between two communities is a **field over the topology**, `χ(x→y)`, anchored by the *relative basket-price of the same real resource* in the two places (an endogenous "commodity bridge" no one can fake without providing real resources). Spending across the network is a **path-payment** routed through that field — the *same* min-cost flow as the [resource market](resource-market.md) — paying the local spread at each hop. The cost of distance is real.
- **There is no "merge" mechanism, and there shouldn't be.** Monetary history shows exchange rates are *discovered*, never decreed: let goods flow across a boundary and the rate is its shadow price. "Merging communities" is the emergent limit (`χ→1`) reached when boundary trade becomes cheap and dense — an [optimal-currency-area](../living-system.md) outcome, not an event.

**Demurrage and UBI are purely local effects that agglomerate.** Each zone runs its own demurrage `δ_z` — shares decay toward the equal share `1/n_eff` unless circulated — recycled as a local UBI drip (weighted by marginal `n_eff`, so it isn't farmable). Summed over an agent's portfolio with a uniform rate, this reproduces the global egalitarian attractor `w_i* = baseline_i + (net flow_i)/δ`: **local dispersion dials agglomerate into one global dispersion bound**, so monetary policy *is* the truth machine's A2 guarantee.

**Leans on:** identity (1, Sybil-proof shares and drip), timestamps (2), the resource market (5, which *defines* the basket each pool is valued in). **Enables:** the resource market (5, denominated in shares), the truth machine (7, dispersed budgets), governance (8). **Co-designed with:** the truth machine — the one dependency *cycle* in the stack.

> ⚠️ **Where it's thin.** Exchange rates are well-anchored only at *thick* boundaries; thin ones are manipulable. Heterogeneous `δ_z` lets the lowest-demurrage zone become a concentration *sink* (a "haven"), so a purely local dial leaks — global dispersion needs a coordinated floor `δ_min`. And a **reserve currency emerges** at the most topologically central zone, re-concentrating power one layer up. **The deepest open question — how zones get *defined*, and how the network sets `δ_min` / a global baseline without a center — is the [remaining crux](../value-as-flow.md#the-remaining-crux).** Part B.
